A NEW DAWN IS ON THE HORIZON.

Recent years has yielded massive leaps forward in the fields of genome sequencing and human genetic research.

  • In 2003, the Human Genome Project ended, the first initiative to completely map the human genome. The project released its first draft of the human genome at the turn of the millennium.
  • In 2015, the American Association for the Advancement of Science awarded its awarded for Breakthrough of the year to CRISPR/Cas9 gene editing.
  • In 2018, scientist He Jiankui edited the CCR5 gene of two human zygotes, to confer HIV resistance on the babies.
  • In 2020, gene editing was successfully used on three American cancer patients.
  • Today, and in the future, humans are sequencing their own genes, both at home and with third party services.

Before Reactive Genetics, even those who have a copy of their genetic data were beholden to experts for critical analysis of the meaning of their genes. Now, average citizens can gain insights into the health implications of their genome. Try it today!
